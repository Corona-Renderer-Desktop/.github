## 🚀 Introduction
Corona Renderer for Windows is a rendering engine that helps you create photorealistic images from your 3D work with a minimum of fuss. It relies on your computer's CPU and integrates with 3ds Max and Cinema 4D, which is why it has become a go-to option for architectural visualization and product rendering. A large part of the appeal of Corona Renderer for 3ds Max is how comfortably it sits within the existing 3ds Max interface, letting you spend your time on lighting and materials rather than configuration. When you begin with a model authored elsewhere, you typically bring it into 3ds Max or Cinema 4D first, and Corona Renderer handles the final image from there.

## ✨ Highlights

### Rendering Quality
Corona Renderer produces physically based lighting, accurate reflections, and clean, believable results that suit close-up architectural and product shots. Because it renders on the CPU, image quality stays predictable and improves as you add processor cores.

### Workflow and Integration
Corona Renderer for 3ds Max and Cinema 4D keeps its materials, lights, and controls where you expect them, and its interactive preview lets you refine a scene as you build it — an approach that rewards experimentation without long waits between changes.

- 8 GB RAM

**Recommended**

## 🎯 Getting Up and Running
1. In 3ds Max or Cinema 4D, set Corona Renderer as the active render engine.
2. Apply Corona materials to your models and place your lights.
3. Start an interactive render to preview lighting and materials in real time.
4. Adjust exposure and shading until the scene reads the way you want.
5. Render the final high-resolution image and save your output.
